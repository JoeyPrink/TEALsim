Colliding Pendulums

This applet shows the collision of two simple penduli bobs, both with the same length of the ruler supporting them. The first one of mass M is at rest at an angle of 90 degrees from vertical. The second one of mass m is at rest at an angle of 0 degrees from vertical. You can set the ratio of M/m by using the slider. When you press play the first bob is released and collides with the second bob. The collision is perfectly elastic, e.g. energy is conserved. The second bob then moves to the left.

Questions: What happens when M/m =1? When M/m << 1? What value of M/m do you need to just get the second bob to the top (180 degrees from vertical) with zero velocity?
