Example_04:

In this example, we create two electromagnetic objects which interact. The first is a charge which is free to move along the vertical axis. The second is a charge which is fixed in space, lying below a "wall" from which the free charge can bounce. Gravity is downward.

We can vary the amount of the fixed-in-space charge to change the height of the free charge above the wall. This simulation has a large friction so that the free charge "settles down" quickly to a equlibrium position when adjusting to changes in the charge on the fixed-in-space charge.

The charge on the fixed-in-space charge can be varied between -10 and 10 charge units, where a charge unit is 2.975 micro Coulombs. The charge on the floating charge is constant at 1 charge unit, its radius is 0.2 meters and its mass is 0.035 kg. The force of gravity on the floating charge, mg, is 0.343 Newtons.

For a fixed-in-space charge of about 4.4 charge units the upward electrostatic repulsion when the floating charge is resting on the wall (center 1 meter above the fixed-in-space charge) will just about balance the downward force of gravity, and the floating charge will levitate above the wall for values of the charge on the fixed-in-space charge above this value.
